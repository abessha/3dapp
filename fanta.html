Fanta

Fanta is a vibrant, fruit-flavored soft drink that originated in Germany during World War II. It is known for its bright colors and a wide range of fruity flavors, such as orange, grape, and strawberry. Fanta is made by combining carbonated water with high-fructose corn syrup or sugar, natural and artificial flavors, and sometimes fruit juice. The drink is carbonated to give it a fizzy texture and is often enjoyed as a refreshing, sweet beverage. Its playful branding and variety of flavors make it a popular choice for those seeking a fruity alternative to traditional cola drinks.
